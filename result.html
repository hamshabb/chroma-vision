About Color Blindness

Color blindness affects at least an estimated 4% of the global population. Rates are higher in some demographics than others, and are more than 10 times higher in males.
Color blindness is caused by defects in the cone cells of the eye-- either inherited (on the X-chromosome) or caused by certain diseases or chemical exposures. There are several types of cone cells, and different types of color blindness associated with each. Red-Green Colorblindness is the most common type (more than one in 15 males in the US has some form of red-green color blindness-- compared with roughly one in 200 females). Color blindness is not curable, but specialized lenses can allow for a greatly increased range of color vision.
In the US, color vision impairment is not covered by the American Disabilities Act.
