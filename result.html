How Common Is Color Blindness?

Red-Green Colorblindness is the most common type (more than one in 15 males in the US has some form of red-green color blindness-- compared with roughly one in 200 females).

What Causes Color Blindess?

Color blindness is caused by defects in the cone cells of the eye-- either inherited (on the X-chromosome) or caused by certain diseases or chemical exposures. There are several types of cone cells, and different types of color blindness associated with each.
